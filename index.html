🔵 Present Perfect 3 pts — Exam

Use when: A past action connected to the present, or a recent event / life experience. Signal words: just · recently · already · yet · ever · never · since · for · successfully
Subject + has / have + past participle (V3)

Forms

FormStructureExample
✅ AffirmativeS + has/have + V3She has finished the report.
❌ NegativeS + hasn't/haven't + V3They haven't arrived yet.
❓ QuestionHas/Have + S + V3?Have you seen this film?

2. The engineers have successfully created (to create / successfully) a highly effective campaign.
3. The company has just launched (launch / just) a groundbreaking product.

🟢 Conditionals — 1st · 2nd · 3rd 3 pts — Exam

1st Conditional — Real / Probable Future

If + Present Simple → will + base verb
Use: A real and possible situation in the future.
If you identify a market need accurately, the product will be more likely to succeed.
If it rains, I will take an umbrella.

2nd Conditional — Hypothetical Present

If + Past Simple → would + base verb
Use: An unreal or imaginary situation in the present.
If you had more resources, you would be able to develop more products.
If I were rich, I would travel the world.

3rd Conditional — Unreal Past / Regret

If + Past Perfect (had + V3) → would have + V3
Use: Something that did NOT happen in the past.
If the team had collaborated more effectively, the project would not have faced so many delays.
If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam.

🟣 Embedded Questions

Definition: A question "hidden" inside a statement or another question. Word order changes to Subject + Verb (SVO) instead of the interrogative order.
Introductory phrase + WH word + Subject + Verb (normal order)
Direct: Where is the bank? → Embedded: Could you tell me where the bank is? ✅

Yes / No Questions → IF or WHETHER

Introductory phrase + if / whether + Subject + Verb
Direct: Do you need help? → Embedded: Could you tell me if you need help? ✅

5 Essential Rules

Rule 1 — Yes/No → if / whether
Can you tell me is it safe? ❌ → Can you tell me if it is safe? ✅
Rule 2 — No contraction at the end
Do you know what time it's? ❌ → Do you know what time it is? ✅
Rule 3 — Punctuation follows the intro
Intro = question → ends with ? | Intro = statement → ends with .
Rule 4 — Same subject → infinitive
I don't know what I should do → I don't know what to do.
She can't decide if she should stay → She can't decide whether to stay.
Rule 5 — "Or not" placement
whether or not ✅ | whether... or not ✅ | if... or not ✅ | if or not... ❌
THINK exception: Never use if/whether after "Do you think". → Do you think they will come? (NOT "do you think if...")

Common Introductory Phrases

Questions (→ ?)Statements (→ .)

Would you mind telling me...I want to know...

🟠 Passive Voice

Use when: The action is more important than who does it, or when the doer is unknown / obvious.
Object + be (conjugated) + Past Participle (V3)
Active: The company launched a product. → Passive: A product was launched by the company.
Active: Engineers develop applications. → Passive: Applications are developed by engineers.

All Tense Forms

Present Simple
am / is / are + V3
The emails are sent every day.
Past Simple
was / were + V3
The report was completed yesterday.
Future
will be + V3
The project will be finished next week.
Present Perfect
has / have been + V3
A new app has been developed.
Modal Verbs
modal + be + V3
The form must be submitted today.
Present Continuous
is / are being + V3
New tools are being developed.

Key Rules

1 — The main verb always becomes V3 (write → written · develop → developed · complete → completed)
2 — "be" changes according to the tense (is / was / has been / will be / must be...)
3 — "by + agent" is optional — drop it when the doer is obvious or unimportant.
Spot passive voice instantly: look for be + V3 → is created · was completed · has been developed · must be signed.

⚖️ vs 💭 Two Essay Types — Key Differences

⚖️ Argumentative Essay

Present BOTH sides fairly. You can give your opinion only in the conclusion.

1
Introduction — Topic + two positions (no personal opinion)
2
Body §1 — FOR — 2-3 arguments + examples
3
Body §2 — AGAINST — 2-3 arguments + examples
4
Conclusion — Summarise both + optional final view
⭐ Both resit topics were argumentative essays!

💭 Opinion Essay

Defend YOUR point of view from the introduction. Refute one counter-argument.

1
Introduction + Thesis — Your clear opinion stated immediately
2
Argument 1 + example
3
Argument 2 + example
4
Counter-argument + Refutation "Some argue... However..."
5
Conclusion — Restate opinion + call to action
Used for leadership, technology, career topics

🔥 Hustle Culture — Productivity vs Burnout

⭐⭐⭐ Direct from Resit Exam · MOST LIKELY

▼
"Some think that hustle culture occasionally fosters productivity and success, while others believe that it ultimately leads to significant negative consequences that outweigh its purported benefits."

Introductions

Introduction A
In today's competitive world, hustle culture — the glorification of overworking — has become a widespread phenomenon. While some argue that it drives ambition and success, others contend that its consequences on health and well-being far outweigh its benefits. This essay will examine both perspectives.
Introduction B
The rise of hustle culture has sparked intense debate in modern society. Proponents claim that working long hours is the key to success, while critics warn that this mentality leads to burnout and a diminished quality of life. This essay will discuss both sides.

✅ Arguments FOR

1. Drives ambition and career growth
For example, many successful entrepreneurs like Elon Musk credit their achievements to an intense work ethic and long working hours.
2. Develops discipline and resilience
Employees who push through challenges develop stronger problem-solving skills and greater mental toughness over time.
3. Accelerates financial independence
Freelancers who take on extra projects can significantly increase their income and reach financial goals faster than their peers.

❌ Arguments AGAINST

1. Leads to burnout and serious health problems
Studies show that employees working over 55 hours per week face a 35% higher risk of stroke and chronic health conditions.
2. Damages personal relationships and social life
People who work around the clock often neglect their families and friends, leading to loneliness and social disconnection.
3. Reduces long-term productivity and creativity
Research shows overworked employees become less creative and make more errors, ultimately harming their professional performance.

Conclusions

Conclusion A
In conclusion, while hustle culture can occasionally boost productivity, its long-term consequences — burnout, health issues, and damaged relationships — suggest that a more balanced approach to work is ultimately more sustainable and beneficial.
Conclusion B
To sum up, although hard work is essential for success, overworking takes a heavy toll on both physical and mental health. Striking a balance between productivity and personal well-being is the real key to lasting achievement.
burnout · productivity · work-life balance · ambition · discipline · stress · mental health · overworking · resilience · exhaustion

👑 Authoritative vs Inspirational Leadership

⭐⭐⭐ Direct from Resit Exam · MOST LIKELY

▼
"While authoritative leadership gives clear rules and consistency, inspirational leadership tends to be more effective for project management."

Introductions

Introduction A
Leadership style plays a critical role in the success of any project. While authoritative leadership provides structure and discipline, inspirational leadership motivates teams through vision and empathy. This essay will explore both approaches and their effectiveness in project management.
Introduction B
The question of which leadership style leads to greater success has been widely debated. Some believe authoritative leadership ensures efficiency, while others argue that inspirational leadership yields better long-term results. This essay will examine both perspectives.

✅ FOR Inspirational Leadership

1. Boosts team motivation and creativity
Leaders who inspire encourage employees to think outside the box and produce innovative solutions that benefit the whole project.
2. Builds trust and stronger team bonds
When leaders lead by example, team members feel valued and are more willing to go the extra mile for the project's success.
3. Increases long-term engagement and satisfaction
Studies show employees under inspirational leaders report higher job satisfaction and significantly lower turnover rates.

During emergencies, quick and decisive leadership is more effective than slow, collaborative consensus-based approaches.

Conclusions

Conclusion A
In conclusion, both leadership styles have their merits. While authoritative leadership ensures structure, inspirational leadership fosters creativity and engagement. The most effective leaders adapt their style to each project's specific needs.
Conclusion B
All things considered, a balanced combination of both styles may be the ideal approach for successful project management. Adaptability remains the true mark of a great leader.

⭐⭐ Lesson 5 — Main Topic

Introductions

Introduction A
With the rise of remote work and high-speed connectivity, the digital nomad lifestyle has become increasingly popular. While it offers freedom and cultural immersion, critics argue it leads to social isolation and professional instability. This essay will examine both the advantages and disadvantages.
Introduction B
The concept of working while travelling the world has attracted millions of professionals in recent years. However, the digital nomad lifestyle is not without its drawbacks. This essay will discuss both the benefits and the challenges of this modern work arrangement.

✅ Arguments FOR

1. Location independence and complete flexibility

🤖 AI in Education — Benefits vs Risks

⭐⭐ Lesson 2 PPT · High Probability

▼
"Some believe AI-assisted tools significantly improve academic performance, while others argue they weaken independent learning and critical thinking. Discuss both views."

Introductions

Introduction A
Artificial intelligence is transforming education at an unprecedented pace. While supporters claim AI tools enhance learning through personalised feedback, critics worry that students may become overly dependent on technology. This essay will explore both sides.
Introduction B
The integration of AI in education has generated significant discussion among educators and students alike. Some argue AI tools boost academic performance, while others caution they hinder critical thinking. This essay will examine both perspectives.

✅ FOR AI in Education

1. Provides a personalised learning experience
AI tools adapt to each student's level, providing targeted exercises and feedback that help weaker students progress faster.
2. Saves teachers' time and improves efficiency
AI can automate grading and administrative tasks, allowing teachers to focus on mentoring and more meaningful interactions.

Students can use AI tutors at any time to clarify doubts, making learning flexible regardless of location or time zone.

The ease of using AI to generate essays raises major questions about plagiarism and academic integrity across all institutions.

Conclusions

Conclusion A
In conclusion, while AI tools offer significant advantages in personalisation and efficiency, they pose real risks to independent learning. Schools should integrate AI as a supplement, not a replacement for traditional education.
Conclusion B
All things considered, AI in education is a powerful tool if used responsibly. Educators must ensure students continue developing their own critical thinking skills alongside technological assistance.

🏠 Remote Work vs Traditional Office Work

▼
"Remote work has become the new norm for many companies. Some see it as the future of work, while others believe traditional office work remains more effective. Discuss both views."

Introductions

Introduction A
Since the tech revolution, remote work has transformed the way millions of people do their jobs. While many employees enjoy the flexibility it provides, some employers argue that in-person collaboration remains essential for productivity. This essay will explore both viewpoints.
Introduction B
The shift toward remote work has sparked a major debate about the future of the workplace. Supporters praise its flexibility and work-life balance benefits, while critics highlight issues of communication and team cohesion. This essay will discuss both sides.

✅ FOR Remote Work

1. Better work-life balance and greater flexibility
Employees save commuting time and can organise their schedules around family needs, leading to higher overall job satisfaction.

🏆 Project Management — Essential or Overrated?

⭐ Lesson 2 — Main Topic

▼
"Some believe rigorous project management is the key factor in delivering successful projects, while others argue that creativity and talent matter more than structured methodologies. Discuss."

Introductions

Introduction A
In today's competitive business world, project management has become an essential discipline. While some argue that structured planning and clear methodologies are the foundation of success, others believe that talent and adaptability matter more. This essay will examine both perspectives.
Introduction B
The question of whether formal project management guarantees success has sparked considerable debate. Supporters argue that planning, timelines, and risk management are indispensable, while critics suggest rigid processes can stifle creativity. This essay will discuss both views.

✅ FOR Project Management

1. Provides clear structure and reduces project risks
Setting clear objectives, timelines, and responsibilities minimises scope creep and ensures every team member understands their role.
2. Improves communication among all stakeholders
Regular updates and progress reports keep all stakeholders informed, preventing misunderstandings and costly project delays.
3. Helps control budgets and meet tight deadlines
Effective cost management and scheduling tools prevent budget overruns and ensure projects are always delivered on time.

❌ AGAINST Rigid Project Management

1. Excessive structure can limit creativity and innovation
Overly rigid methodologies like strict Waterfall processes can prevent teams from adapting quickly to new opportunities or market changes.
2. Bureaucracy can significantly slow execution
In fast-moving industries, spending too much time on planning documents and approval processes can cause teams to fall behind competitors.
3. Project success often depends on people, not processes
Many successful startups have thrived through talented, agile teams rather than through formal project management frameworks.

Conclusions

Conclusion A
In conclusion, while project management provides essential structure and risk control, it must be balanced with flexibility and creativity. The best teams combine disciplined planning with the agility to adapt when circumstances change unexpectedly.
Conclusion B
To sum up, effective project management is not about rigid control but about empowering teams to deliver results. When applied thoughtfully, it remains one of the most powerful tools for organisational success.

Introductions

Introduction A
Professional ghosting — the act of suddenly ending communication in a work context without explanation — has become a growing trend in modern recruitment and workplaces. While some consider it a practical way to avoid awkward conversations, others view it as deeply unprofessional. This essay examines both perspectives.
Introduction B

✅ Arguments FOR (understanding the behaviour)

Conclusion A

Body Paragraph 1 — FOR Technology Improving Balance: On the one hand, technology has introduced considerable improvements to the way professionals manage their working lives. Furthermore, remote workers frequently find themselves working around the clock because the physical separation between their workspace and personal living space has completely disappeared, causing the work-life boundary to become dangerously blurry. Consequently, studies consistently link excessive screen time to significantly increased levels of stress, anxiety, and sleep disorders — all of which take a serious toll on long-term personal well-being. Conclusion: In conclusion, while technology offers extraordinary potential to improve professional efficiency and personal flexibility, its negative impact on work-life balance cannot be overlooked. The solution lies not in rejecting technology entirely, but in using it mindfully, establishing clear digital boundaries, and ensuring that organisations actively respect their employees' personal time outside working hours.

📝 Model Essay — Professional Ghosting

Introduction: Professional ghosting — the act of abruptly ending all communication in a work context without providing any explanation — has emerged as a troubling trend in modern recruitment and workplace culture. While some argue it represents a legitimate personal boundary in an era of digital overload, others view it as deeply unprofessional and damaging to trust. This essay will explore both perspectives. Body Paragraph 1 — Understanding the Behaviour: On the one hand, there are understandable reasons why professional ghosting has become so widespread. First, some individuals lack the communication skills or emotional confidence to navigate difficult conversations such as rejection or resignation, and they resort to silence as a way of avoiding discomfort. Moreover, many candidates who ghost employers do so as a direct response to having experienced the same treatment themselves — companies that never responded to their applications effectively normalised this behaviour first. In addition, the increasingly informal and asynchronous nature of digital communication has blurred the traditional boundaries of professional courtesy across different generations. Body Paragraph 2 — The Negative Consequences: On the other hand, the damaging consequences of professional ghosting are significant and long-lasting. For instance, disappearing without explanation permanently burns bridges and destroys hard-earned professional trust — in a relatively small professional world, such reputational damage can be extremely difficult to recover from. Furthermore, candidates or colleagues who are left without any feedback or acknowledgement often experience serious anxiety, which takes a heavy toll on their mental health, confidence, and overall well-being. Consequently, when companies routinely ghost applicants, they signal a fundamental lack of respect that severely damages their employer brand and makes it significantly harder to attract top talent in the future. Conclusion: In conclusion, while the pressures and informality of modern professional life may help explain the rise of professional ghosting, they do not justify it in any meaningful way. Clear, honest, and respectful communication must remain the standard in all professional contexts. Ultimately, the ability to deliver difficult messages with empathy and transparency is a mark of genuine professionalism and strong character.

📝 Model Essay — Project Management vs Creativity

Introduction: The question of whether formal project management or individual creativity and talent is the more important factor in delivering successful projects has been widely debated in professional and academic circles alike. Body Paragraph 1 — FOR Project Management: On the one hand, formal project management provides a critical foundation that significantly increases the likelihood of project success. Moreover, effective budget management and systematic scheduling tools prevent costly budget overruns and help teams consistently meet even the tightest of deadlines. Consequently, in rapidly evolving industries where adaptability is paramount, the ability to think outside the box and pivot quickly often determines success far more decisively than any formal planning tool. Conclusion: All things considered, both rigorous project management and creative talent play essential and complementary roles in delivering truly successful projects.
