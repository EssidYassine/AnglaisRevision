🔵 Present Perfect

Grammar
Quand l'utiliser : Action passée avec un lien au présent, ou action récente/expérience de vie. Souvent avec : just, already, yet, recently, ever, never, since, for.
Subject + has/have + past participle (V3)

Formes

FormeStructureExemple
✅ AffirmativeS + has/have + V3She has finished the report.
❌ NegativeS + has/have + not + V3They haven't arrived yet.
❓ QuestionHas/Have + S + V3?Have you seen this movie?

Mots-clés importants

AdverbePositionExemple
recentlyavant le V3 ou en finOur team has recently developed a new app.
justentre has/have et V3The company has just launched a product.
alreadyentre has/have et V3I have already finished my work.
yeten fin (négatif/question)They haven't started yet.
successfullyentre has/have et V3Engineers have successfully created a campaign.
never / everentre has/have et V3I have never been to Japan.
L'ordre des adverbes est clé à l'examen ! "has recently developed" — l'adverbe va ENTRE has/have et le participe passé.

Exemples de l'examen

Our team has recently developed (recently / to develop) a new app.
The engineers have successfully created (to create / successfully) a campaign.
The company has just launched (launch / just) a groundbreaking product.

🟢 Conditionals (1st, 2nd, 3rd)

Grammar

1st Conditional — Situation réelle / probable (futur)

If + present simple → will + base verb
Usage : Parler d'une situation réelle et possible dans le futur.
If you identify a market need accurately, the product will be more likely to succeed.
If it rains, I will take an umbrella.

2nd Conditional — Situation imaginaire / hypothétique (présent)

If + past simple → would + base verb
Usage : Parler d'une situation irréelle ou imaginaire au présent.
If you had more resources, you would be able to develop more products.
If I were rich, I would travel the world.

3rd Conditional — Situation irréelle dans le passé (regret)

If + past perfect (had + V3) → would have + V3
Usage : Parler d'une situation irréelle dans le passé — quelque chose qui ne s'est PAS passé.
If the team had collaborated more effectively, the project would not have faced so many delays.
If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam.

Résumé rapide

TypeIf clauseMain clauseQuand ?
1stPresent Simplewill + VFutur probable
2ndPast Simplewould + VPrésent imaginaire
3rdPast Perfect (had+V3)would have + V3Passé irréel
Astuce rapide : had → would have (3rd), had → would (2nd), present → will (1st). Regarde le verbe dans la clause IF pour identifier le type !

🟣 Embedded Questions

Grammar
Définition : Une question "cachée" à l'intérieur d'une phrase déclarative ou d'une autre question. L'ordre des mots change : on utilise l'ordre SVO (sujet + verbe) au lieu de l'ordre interrogatif.

Règle principale

Introductory phrase + WH word + Subject + Verb (ordre normal)
Direct: Where is the bank? (V + S)
Embedded: Could you tell me where the bank is? (S + V) ✅

Pour les questions Yes/No → utiliser IF ou WHETHER

Introductory phrase + if/whether + Subject + Verb
Direct: Do you need help?
Embedded: Could you tell me if you need help? ✅

5 Règles essentielles

1. Yes/No → if / whether
Can you tell me is it safe? ❌
Can you tell me if/whether it is safe? ✅
2. Pas de contraction en fin de clause
Do you know what time it's? ❌
Do you know what time it is? ✅
3. Point d'interrogation si l'intro est une question
Could you tell me where the bank is? (intro = question → ?)
I don't know where the bank is. (intro = statement → .)
4. Même sujet → infinitif possible
I don't know what I should do → I don't know what to do.
She can't decide if she should stay → She can't decide whether to stay.
5. "Or not" placement
Avec whether: "whether or not" ✅ OU "whether... or not" ✅
Avec if: seulement "if... or not" ✅ (jamais "if or not..." ❌)
Exception avec THINK : Pas de if/whether après "Do you think". → Do you think they will come? (pas "do you think if they will come")

Phrases d'introduction courantes

Questions (→ ?)Statements (→ .)
Do you know...I don't know...
Could you tell me...I wonder...
Would you mind telling me...I want to know...
Do you remember...I'm curious about...
Do you think...I'm interested in knowing...

🟠 Passive Voice

Grammar
Définition : On utilise la voix passive quand l’action est plus importante que la personne qui fait l’action.
Object + be + Past Participle (V3)

📌 Active → Passive

Active: The company launched a product.
Passive: A product was launched by the company.
Active: Engineers develop applications.
Passive: Applications are developed by engineers.

🧠 Structures principales

Tense Structure Example
Present Simple am/is/are + V3 The emails are sent every day.
Past Simple was/were + V3 The report was completed yesterday.
Future will be + V3 The project will be finished next week.
Present Perfect has/have been + V3 A new app has been developed.
Modal Verbs modal + be + V3 The form must be submitted today.

⚠️ Règles importantes

1. Le verbe principal devient V3
write → written
develop → developed
complete → completed
2. Le verbe "be" change selon le temps
is / was / has been / will be...
3. "By + agent" est optionnel
The project was completed by the team.
→ souvent supprimé si inutile.

🎯 Expressions fréquentes à l'examen

The meeting was postponed.
A solution has been found.
New technologies are being developed.
The documents must be signed.
Astuce : repère toujours le verbe BE + participe passé (V3). Exemple : is created, was completed, has been developed.

🔴 Idioms & Expressions

Vocabulary

Tous les idioms des cours (L5 Digital Nomads + L2 Project Management + Exam resit). Apprends le sens ET un exemple d'utilisation.

🔥 To burn bridges
Détruire une relation ou une opportunité de façon irréversible.
"She completely burned bridges when she stopped replying."
💰 To pay the piper
Faire face aux conséquences de ses actions passées.
"He ignored visa rules and now he's paying the piper."
⏰ To work around the clock
Travailler jour et nuit, sans arrêt.
"The team had to work around the clock to meet the deadline."
🧳 To live out of a suitcase
Voyager constamment sans domicile fixe.
"Digital nomads quickly get used to living out of a suitcase."
🐀 The rat race
La routine stressante et compétitive du travail moderne.
"Many employees want to escape the rat race."
🥊 To roll with the punches
S'adapter aux situations difficiles, rester flexible.
"When problems happen, nomads must learn to roll with the punches."
⛵ To rock the boat
Causer des problèmes ou des conflits dans une situation stable.
"She didn't want to rock the boat at her new job."
🍇 To hear through the grapevine
Entendre une information de façon informelle (bouche-à-oreille).
"I heard through the grapevine that layoffs are coming."
💔 To take a toll (on)
Avoir des conséquences négatives importantes.
"The constant stress took a heavy toll on his health."
🧱 Talking to a dead wall
Parler à quelqu'un qui ne répond pas / n'écoute pas.
"Getting my kids to clean is like talking to a dead wall."
À l'examen, on te demandera soit de matcher les idioms à leurs définitions, soit de compléter des phrases avec le bon idiom. Apprends les contextes d'utilisation !

In recent years, the digital nomad lifestyle has become increasingly popular due to technological advancements and remote work opportunities.
Introduction 2:
Nowadays, many professionals choose to work remotely while travelling around the world in search of freedom and flexibility.
Positive Arguments:
  • Digital nomads enjoy greater flexibility and independence.
  • Travelling allows people to discover different cultures and lifestyles.
  • Remote work reduces commuting stress and saves time.
  • Nomads often improve communication and adaptability skills.
  • Working online creates international career opportunities.
Negative Arguments:
  • Constant travelling may create financial instability.
  • Many digital nomads suffer from loneliness and isolation.
  • Internet connectivity problems can affect productivity.
  • Time zone differences complicate teamwork.
  • It can be difficult to separate work from personal life.
flexibility — remote work — independence — productivity — connectivity — mobility — freelance
Conclusion 1:
To conclude, the digital nomad lifestyle offers freedom and exciting experiences, but it also requires discipline and organization.
Conclusion 2:
From my perspective, digital nomadism can be highly rewarding if individuals know how to manage their responsibilities effectively.

Nowadays, people are surrounded by too many choices, which can make decision-making more stressful and complicated.
Introduction 2:
Although having many options seems beneficial, excessive choices may create confusion and anxiety.

  • People can choose what suits them best.
  • Consumers have more freedom and opportunities.
  • Competition improves product quality.
  • Individuals can explore different possibilities.
  • More choices increase personal satisfaction sometimes.
Negative Effects:
  • Too many choices create stress and confusion.
  • People fear making the wrong decision.
  • Decision fatigue reduces productivity.
  • Many individuals overthink simple situations.
  • FOBO (Fear Of Better Options) becomes common.
Conclusion:
In conclusion, having many choices can be beneficial, but people should learn to make decisions confidently and efficiently.
